Introduction

Face manipulation in photographs is a complex task that often encounters significant challenges, particularly when employing traditional latent space techniques. These methods frequently result in noticeable artifacts and inconsistencies in identity, which can detract from the realism and utility of the manipulated images. In this paper, we introduce a novel framework called Domain Transfer in Latent Space (DTLS) for facial attribute manipulation, which addresses these issues by redefining the manipulation process.

Our approach is a novel structure with much more accuracy and efficiency, as distinct domains within the DTLS framework. It enables smooth transitions between these domains, and allows for precise and fine-grained control over facial modifications while ensuring the preservation of the subject’s identity.

Model structure

Our approach proposed defines different domains representing varying degree of specific image attribute. We take smile attribute as an example, we set the domains by the level of smiling. For example, no smile as domain , and the deeper domain have larger smile attribute. This is an overview for our model. We use U-Net structure to do the transformation work.
